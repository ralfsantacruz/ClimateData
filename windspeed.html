Latitude and Wind speed

The closer you are to the earth's poles, expect higher winds.

Figure

Most wind speeds on earth don't go above 10 mph.

Summary

Wind speed is usually a product of atmospheric pressure differences. Geographic location is a very important aspect of how windy a particular place will be. It's difficult to generalize wind speed for regions that are closer to the equator as mountain ranges can play a big role in generating these pressure differences.

However, as we move further and further from the equator, we see a trend where there are more and more locations with higher average wind speeds. As we move close to the poles, the temperature becomes more predictable and there is a trend toward higher wind speeds in frigid areas of the planet.
