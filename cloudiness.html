Latitude and Cloudiness

Stay within 0 and 40 degrees for the clearest skies on the planet - head north for covered skies.

Figure

Cloudiness is a tricky metric

Summary

Cloudiness is a very variable statistic to measure - it can be different for any city on any given day. However, there are some general trend that can be discovered even on one given day.

Many data points along latitudes of 0 and 40 have generally clear skies while the Northern Hemisphere contains a very high concentration of datapoints where there is significant cloudiness. Winter is a major player in this phenomenon.

Looking at the lower latitudes, we see that there are less instances of cloudy skies, but there are not enough data points to conclude that there is a significant trend going on between location and cloudiness.
