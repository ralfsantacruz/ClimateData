Latitude and Humidity

There are few places on Earth where humidity drops below 60%.

Figure

There is more variability in humidty in the Northern Hemisphere.

Summary

When looking at humidty compareed to latitude, it's clear that most places on earth are relatively humid. Some outliers where the humidity drops below 60% are mostly deserts.

There seems to be less variability in humidty around the equator and as we move towards the poles, there are more diferences in humidity. Temperature and ocean currents are responsible for changes in humidty and it's known that in the northern and southern hemispheres, currents flow from north to south, while in the equator the currents move from east to west which can explain the constancy of the equator's humidity.
